A — Asymmetric Split

Text anchored left, constellation floats right. Strong asymmetry creates visual tension. The skill nodes in the background are interactive — they drift slowly, respond to cursor proximity (gravitational pull), and glow on hover to reveal related projects. The constellation IS your skill map, but it doesn't demand attention — it rewards curiosity.

B — Centered Orbital

You're at the center, skills orbit around you. Text is centered and dramatic — a bold statement that fills the viewport. The orbital rings create a sense of a solar system with you as the sun. Skill nodes slowly orbit on elliptical paths and respond to cursor. More immersive and spatial, but less asymmetric tension. Better mobile experience since everything is centered.

My take: I lean toward A (Asymmetric) — it creates stronger visual hierarchy and avoids the "centered hero" pattern that your current site already uses. The left-anchored text gives the eye a clear starting point, and the constellation on the right rewards exploration. It also transitions more naturally into the bento grid below. But B is better on mobile since centering is more natural on narrow screens — we can make A responsive by centering on mobile.
